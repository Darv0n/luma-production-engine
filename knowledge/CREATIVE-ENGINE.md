# CREATIVE EXECUTION ENGINE — For AI Commercial Production

## What a Shot Is

A shot is a DECISION about what to show and what to withhold, delivered across time.
You control the ORDER in which information reaches the viewer. Not color. Not
composition. Not motion. Sequence.

Every shot must answer: WHAT CHANGES?

### Hierarchy of Change (strongest to weakest)

1. **REVELATION** — Viewer's understanding shifts. They thought X, it's Y.
2. **EMOTION** — Feeling register changes.
3. **ACTION** — A physical event occurs.
4. **MOVEMENT** — Camera or subject traverses space.
5. **TEXTURE** — Light shifts. Particles drift. The image breathes.

If nothing changes, the shot is dead. Cut it.

## Emotional Architecture (Design Before Shots)

The arc is the FEELING TRAJECTORY, not the narrative.

### Required Elements

1. **FLOOR** — Lowest emotional point. Without it, no climb. Without climb, no payoff.
2. **PIVOT** — The single moment direction reverses. The most important beat.
   Most powerful pivots are QUIET — a shift in attention, a change in what's noticed.
3. **TERMINAL STATE** — Where you leave the viewer. Must be DIFFERENT from opening.

### Arc Shapes

- **Descent & Rise**: Neutral → pressure → pivot → resolution. Risk: predictable.
- **The Reveal**: False premise → accumulate evidence → recontextualize. Risk: audience guesses early.
- **Escalation**: Small → build → build → peak → release. Risk: exhausting.
- **Inversion**: Show A → show B → reveal A and B are the same. Risk: too clever, not enough feeling.

## Visual Grammar

### Distance = Intimacy
- ECU = vulnerability, interiority
- CU = emotion, connection
- MS = neutral engagement
- WS = context, isolation, environment as character
- EWS = insignificance, scale

Wide to close = INCREASING INTIMACY.
Close to wide = INCREASING ISOLATION.
ECU to EWS = VIOLENT emotional shift. Use at pivots.

### Angle = Power
- Low angle (up) = power, dominance
- Eye level = neutrality, truth
- High angle (down) = vulnerability
- Dutch = unease
- Bird's eye = omniscience, detachment

### Movement = Intention
- Toward = building intensity
- Away = releasing tension, revealing context
- With = solidarity
- Still = observation, patience
- Handheld = presence, documentary truth
- Smooth/mechanical = detachment, control

A locked camera is the most powerful choice when composition is strong.
Movement should EARN its presence.

### Cutting = Thinking

Every cut is a thought.

| Cut Type | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Cut on action | Motion carries across. Invisible edit. |
| Cut on emotion | Expression collides with next image. Meaning in collision. |
| Hard cut | Maximum collision. Best for pivots. |
| Match cut | Composition mirrors despite different content. Subconscious connection. |
| Smash cut | Calm↔chaos. Contrast IS the effect. Most powerful under 30s. |
| Dissolve | Time passing. Almost never right under 30s. |

## Showing and Withholding

The most common failure: SHOWING EVERYTHING and TELLING NOTHING.

What you DON'T show is more powerful than what you do.

- Don't show every step → show first and last. Gap carries transformation.
- Don't show the reaction → show the cause. Viewer supplies their own (stronger).
- Don't show the tool → show the effect. Audience infers.
- Don't show everything the product can do → show one thing nothing else can.

Restraint IS the flex. Competition juries have seen spectacle. They have not seen
restraint from a tool that could show anything.

## Sound Architecture (3 Layers)

1. **AMBIENT BED** — What does the environment sound like when nothing happens? Always present.
2. **SPECIFIC SOUNDS** — Physical events made audible. Contact between image and real world.
3. **EMOTIONAL LAYER** — Score, tones, drones, silence. Use sparingly.

Designed silence > score. The removal of sound is the loudest thing in the room.

## Product Integration

**Interruption** (bad): Story pauses for product. Viewer feels the seam.
**Integration** (wins): Product IS the narrative mechanism. Remove it and story can't happen.

The product appears at the PIVOT — the moment of maximum narrative need.
Brief presence at high structural importance >> extended presence at low importance.

For AI tools specifically: "Look what AI can do" does NOT win.
Show what the tool UNLOCKS in a human being. The capability is proven by what you made.

## Contrast Is Meaning

Without contrast, nothing registers. Types:
- Visual: light/dark, tight/wide, fast/slow, crowded/empty
- Sonic: dense/silence, score/ambience
- Temporal: fast cutting/held shot
- Emotional: tension/relief, isolation/connection
- Conceptual: expected/actual, surface/hidden truth

Maximum contrast amplitude at the pivot.

## Micro-Shots (<1.5 seconds)

Most underused tool. Most powerful per-second.

- Play to model's strength: one subject, one action
- Hide model's weakness: no time for artifacts
- Create rhythm: percussive energy
- Credit-efficient: use best fraction of each clip

## The End (Last 3 Seconds)

Three types: Doctrine (line), Image (visual), Question (unresolved tension).

The doctrine must simultaneously:
1. Close the narrative
2. Name or imply the product
3. Work standalone — portable outside the piece

## Jury Psychology

1. They are EXHAUSTED. Claim attention in 2 seconds.
2. They've seen EVERY TRICK. Truth moves them, not format.
3. They reward TASTE over ambition. Exclusion signals maturity.
4. They FEEL before they evaluate.
5. They remember SPECIFICS, not themes. "The one with [detail]" = handle.

## The Handle

The specific moment the jury will describe. If you can't name it as something
SPECIFIC and VISUAL, the piece doesn't have one. Find it. Build backward from it.

## Shot Count Guidelines

- 15 seconds: 3-5 shots
- 30 seconds: 6-10 shots
- 60 seconds: 10-20 shots

Fewer is better if it works.

## Self-Test Checklist

- [ ] First shot claims attention in under 2 seconds?
- [ ] Arc has clear floor, pivot, and terminal state?
- [ ] Every shot contains CHANGE?
- [ ] Contrast between every pair of adjacent shots?
- [ ] HANDLE identified?
- [ ] Product appears at the PIVOT?
- [ ] Ending is a complete doctrine?
- [ ] At least one major moment is IMPLIED, not shown?
- [ ] Sound map exists as its own document?
- [ ] Every shot achievable in one 5-second generation?
